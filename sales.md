# .claude/rules/sales.md — Sales Coaching Rules

## NEPQ Framework Overview
NEPQ (Neuro-Emotional Persuasion Questioning) is the conversation framework used for all patient plan consultations. It is question-based, not pitch-based. The WC leads with questions that help the patient identify their own wellness needs.

## NEPQ Conversation Stages

### Stage 1: Connection Questions
Purpose: Build rapport, understand why the patient came in today
Approach: Open-ended, non-clinical
Examples:
- "What brought you in to see us today?"
- "What's been going on with your wellness routine lately?"
- "Tell me more about what you've been experiencing."

### Stage 2: Situation Questions
Purpose: Understand current state — how long, how often, what impact
Examples:
- "How long has this been affecting you?"
- "How often does this come up in your daily life?"
- "How has this been impacting your work / sleep / activity?"

### Stage 3: Consequence Questions
Purpose: Surface the deeper impact — what happens if this continues
Examples:
- "What do you think happens if this doesn't get addressed?"
- "How does this affect the things that are most important to you?"
- "What's the cost to you if this continues for another year?"

### Stage 4: Solution Questions
Purpose: Let the patient articulate what they want
Examples:
- "If you could have consistent wellness support, what would that mean for you?"
- "What would it look like to have this as part of your regular routine?"

### Stage 5: Plan Recommendation
Present the plan that best fits based on signals from Stages 1-4.

## Plan Recommendation Logic by Patient Signal

| Patient Signal | Recommended Plan |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "I want to come in regularly" + budget flexible | Annual Wellness Plan |
| "I want to try it first" | Monthly Wellness Plan |

## The 4 Core Objection Scripts

### Objection 1: "I need to think about it"
Response Framework:
- Acknowledge: "Of course, this is a decision about your wellness — take whatever time you need."
- Clarify: "Is there a specific piece of information that would help you decide?"
- If hesitant: "What would you need to feel confident about moving forward?"
- Do NOT pressure. If patient needs time, schedule a follow-up visit.

### Objection 2: "It's too expensive"
Response Framework:
- Reframe: "Let's look at the per-visit value. Monthly Wellness works out to [X] per adjustment."
- Compare to status quo: "What does it cost you to not address this?"
- Offer Monthly: "The monthly plan lets you start without a long-term commitment."
- Never disparage the plan pricing or suggest it's negotiable.

### Objection 3: "I'm not sure I'll use it enough"
Response Framework:
- Understand cadence preference: "How often were you thinking of coming in?"
- Educate on consistency: "Most members find that [2x/month] is where they feel the difference."
- Offer Monthly to start: "The monthly plan is a great way to build the habit and see how it fits."

### Objection 4: "I already have a chiropractor"
Response Framework:
- Acknowledge: "That's great that you're already prioritizing your spine health."
- Differentiate: "The Joint is designed for convenient, consistent maintenance care — many members use us alongside their regular care."
- No negative comparisons to other providers.
- Soft invitation: "We'd love for you to try a visit and see if our routine works for you."

## Rules for Sales Scripts
- Never make clinical outcome promises in a sales script
- Never mention specific competitor pricing
- Always let the patient lead — NEPQ is question-driven, not pitch-driven
- All plan details must match the approved plans in knowledge/02_products_and_plans.md
- If a patient mentions a medical condition during the sales conversation, redirect to WC clinical scope — do not provide clinical advice
- Workers' Comp patients: NEVER pitch plans. They are covered under WC protocol.
